Philosophy

As a family nurse practitioner (FNP), I am grounded in the values and principles of nursing, as well as the unique role and responsibilities of the NP. The emphasis of patient-centered care, holistic assessment and treatment, as well as advocating for my patients is at the core of practice. Finally, collaboration with other professional safeguards against missed opportunities for successful healthcare outcomes. I am devoted to working with other for the benefit of my patients.

I am committed to providing patient-centered care. Prioritizing each patientâ€™s individual needs, desires, and values in regards to their care is paramount when making collaborative healthcare decisions. This is crucial to developing and building trust in my relationship with patients as well as ensuring they are involved in the direction of their own healthcare.

From a holistic standpoint, I am steadfast in understanding that not only physical symptoms, but emotional, social, and spiritual well-being are imperative when considering patient health. I believe that healthcare is not only focused on treating illness or disease, but overall health. Additionally, health promotion and disease prevention are at the forefront of my practice.

As a previous advocate to women in crisis, I have learned the benefit of advocacy. Advocacy is a key component in my FNP philosophy. I will remain dedicated to advocating for patients, as well as ensuring the received the best possible care. This is inclusive of healthcare services as well as for their rights and personal healthcare preferences and decisions.

Finally, collaboration with other healthcare professionals is essential in my practice. Working alongside other healthcare professionals, in addition to the patients and their families, expands the possibilities for positive healthcare outcomes. A comprehensive healthcare plan is critical within patient care. I understand the best possible outcomes are only achieved by inclusion, collaboration, and a team approach for all patients.

By staying grounded in the principles of nursing as well as underscoring the importance of patient-centered, holistic, advocacy-based and collaborative care. I strive to be an effective, well-rounded, accepting, and strong advocate for patients as they navigate through their lifelong healthcare.
