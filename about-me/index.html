About Me

My name is Ann Bruer. I am a certified family nurse practitioner. I live in Kansas City, Missouri. I am originally from Springfield, Missouri. Before going to nursing school, I had a career in victim advocacy for approximately 15 years, mostly working in the intimate partner violence field. When I turned 40, I decided to change my career and started the long process of my journey. I took courses to become an EMT through the University of Missouri-Kansas City School of Medicine in the evenings after work. Upon completing this certification, I started working at University Health, Lakewood Emergency Department as a patient care technician while I completed pre-requisite courses for nursing school. As I had already attained my Bachelors’ of Science from Missouri State University, I was able to apply to Research College of Nursing’s Accelerated Option Program. This enabled me to complete my Bachelors’ of Science in Nursing in one short (but busy) year. As a single mother of two adolescents during this time, I was driven to succeed not only for myself but for my children. I completed my Bachelors’ of Science in Nursing in 2019. Afterwards, I immediately applied for, and was accepted into, the Research College of Nursing Master’s Program for Family Nurse Practitioners. While attending school, I began working at North Kanas City Hospital in the Float Pool. While I love working in the emergency department, I felt like this opportunity would broaden my knowledge in healthcare. I have worked with orthopedic, oncology, rehabilitation, telemetry, and neurology patients. This experience definitely expanded my understanding of chronic health conditions as well as ways in which to treat them.

I believe I have a unique understanding of patient care, as I am a congenital heart defect patient. I was born with Transposition of the Great Arteries and have received three open heart surgeries (all before age three). I am a patient at a local cardiology office and see my cardiologist at least once a year. As one of the first patients to receive a surgical procedure that saved my life as an infant, my future cardiac health is somewhat of a mystery. As a result, I can emphasize with patients in the “unknowing” of their personal health care throughout their lifetimes. In addition, during my Bachelors’ of Science in Nursing Program, my fiancé was diagnosed with Stage IV lung cancer as a result of being exposed to burn pits during his station in Afghanistan. Consequently, I have learned that life can hand you challenges that quickly changes one’s perspective.

I am now married to my husband and he is doing well. I have two children, Hannah and Charlie as well as two step daughters, Sierra and Averi. I am also a step grandmother to Jameison and Liam. Finally, I have three dogs, Miller, Laila, and Chico. I enjoy spending time with my family (including the dogs), traveling, and watching anything that is true crime documentaries.
